# Purple River Brand Guidelines

> For contributors, designers, and copywriters. These rules were established by the founders in a brand workshop. Follow them precisely — they are not suggestions.

---

## Voice & Copy Rules — NON-NEGOTIABLE

### 1. Mission as invitation, not rescue.
Frame everything as "join the current," never "let us save you." Purple River is not a hero riding to the rescue. The mission partner is the protagonist.

**Wrong**: "Let us rescue your organization from cyber threats."
**Right**: "Join the current — security built around your mission."

---

### 2. "We and our" language — community on the same side.
The community, the mission, and the organization are on the same side. Write from inside that alliance, not from outside looking in.

**Wrong**: "Your organization faces these threats."
**Right**: "Organizations like ours face these threats."

---

### 3. Story first, data second.
Open every section with a person, a moment, or a scenario. Statistics validate the story — they don't replace it. If you lead with a number, rewrite it.

**Wrong**: "1.8M non-profits face enterprise-level threats with no security budget."
**Right**: "Maria runs a food bank with 12 staff. Last October, a volunteer's account was compromised. The attacker had access for four months. [*Then*] 1.8 million U.S. non-profits sit in the same position."

---

### 4. Radical transparency.
Share real numbers even when they're small. Acknowledge limitations. Show process, not just outcomes. "We're working on this" is a valid and honest answer.

---

### 5. No hero language.
Purple River is **not** the hero of the story. The mission partner is.

**Never write:**
- "We save"
- "We rescue"
- "We protect you from"
- "We keep you safe"

**Write instead:**
- "We work alongside"
- "We help you close the gap"
- "We build with you"

---

### 6. No over-promising.
Never write:
- "100% secure"
- "Bulletproof"
- "Guaranteed protection"
- "Eliminate risk"

Security reduces and manages risk. It does not eliminate it.

---

### 7. No humor in serious moments.
Cybersecurity stakes are real — lost donor data, compromised beneficiary records, breached shelter records. No jokes. No memes. No cute taglines. Warmth and clarity are fine. Levity at the wrong moment is not.

---

### 8. No stock-photo humans.
Do not use:
- Generic stock photography with smiling people at laptops
- Hooded-hacker imagery
- Isometric tech illustrations
- AI-generated faces

Use instead:
- Abstract gradient compositions
- The river-flow motif
- Real community photography (only with explicit permission)
- Bold typography as a visual element

---

### 9. Memorable enough to repeat.
Every sentence should be writable on a whiteboard. No corporate-speak.

**Never write:**
- "committed to empowering stakeholders"
- "leverage synergies"
- "holistic approach to digital transformation"
- "best-in-class solutions"

**Write instead:**
- Short declarative sentences
- Active voice
- Specific nouns (not "solutions," but "MFA" or "access review")

---

### 10. Mission as a verb.
Every CTA is active. Not passive. Not vague.

**Weak CTAs:**
- "Learn More"
- "Contact Us"
- "Get Started"

**Strong CTAs:**
- "Join the Waitlist"
- "Start the Conversation"
- "Reach Out"
- "Get Notified"

## Color System

| Token | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| `--pr-indigo-950` | `#0A0B14` | Primary background (near-black) |
| `--pr-indigo-900` | `#14132B` | Dark surface / nav |
| `--pr-indigo-800` | `#1E1B4B` | Card backgrounds |
| `--pr-blue-600` | `#4F46E5` | Primary brand — interactive elements |
| `--pr-blue-500` | `#6366F1` | Hover states |
| `--pr-cyan-400` | `#22D3EE` | Bright accent — highlights, focus |
| `--pr-cyan-300` | `#67E8F9` | Glow / focus rings |
| `--pr-lavender-300` | `#C4B5FD` | Ribbon highlight — use sparingly |
| `--pr-lavender-200` | `#DDD6FE` | Soft surfaces |
| `--pr-fog-50` | `#F5F3FF` | Light section backgrounds |
| `--pr-ink-900` | `#0F172A` | Body text on light |
| `--pr-ink-500` | `#64748B` | Secondary / metadata text |

**Rules:**
- Default theme is **dark**. Light sections are used strategically for contrast.
- Gradients flow **indigo → cyan** with lavender as a highlight — never flat purple.
- Lavender is a highlight color. Do not use it as a primary fill or background.
- All text must meet **WCAG AA contrast minimum** (4.5:1 for body, 3:1 for large text).

---

## Typography

- **Display / Headings**: Space Grotesk, weight 600–700, tight tracking
- **Body**: Inter, weight 400–500
- **Maximum two type families**
- Use generous size jumps: `text-5xl md:text-7xl` for hero headings

## Logo Usage

| File | Use When |
|------|----------|
| `purple_river_logo_dark.PNG` | On dark backgrounds |
| `purple_river_logo_light.PNG` | On light backgrounds |

Minimum display height: 28px. Never stretch, recolor, or rotate the logo.

## The River Metaphor — How to Use It

The river is:
- **Continuous**: Security isn't a project with an end date.
- **Directional**: Intelligence flows both ways — into the community and back.
- **Relational**: We're here for the long journey alongside the mission partner.

Reference the metaphor in: hero sections, CTAs, closing copy. Do not overuse it — it lands best when it appears once or twice per page, not in every sentence.
